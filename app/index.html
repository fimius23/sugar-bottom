About

It can be troubling to think that you have a problem you can not control or handle yourself. It can be worrisome to think that you or your child is going down a behavioral path that will be difficult to change. It can be extremely stressful to have feelings of low self-worth or thoughts of harming yourself. These are just a few examples of situations in which professional mental health therapy is necessary.

Taking the first step to seek help with your life concerns can be confusing and challenging. As a therapist, I under- stand that it takes a giant of leap of faith to trust a stranger with the most important and often most painful areas of your life. The purpose of this website is to answer some of the most common questions that you might have about psychotherapy and to help you decide if I might be a good fit for you in your search for support.

My belief is that you can grow, heal, and transform-no matter how intense or difficult your experiences have been. We will work together to create a comfortable and safe relationship where vulnerable issues can be explored and understood together.

Bio

I am a clinical therapist providing brief, solution-focused therapy and long-term psychotherapy for adults and children. I also provide counseling for families. I work with people to address a broad range of concerns including depression, anxiety, behavioral problems, relationship & identity issues, life transitions, anger, and trauma.

I have helped families negotiate life changes and adjustments and the negative behaviors that can be a result of those changes. I create a comfortable and safe relationship where vulnerable issues can be explored and understood together.

I am licensed by the state of Nebraska as a Licensed Independent Clinical Social Worker and Master Social Worker. I hold a Master's Degree in Social Work from Loyola University Chicago and a Master's Degree in Child Development from the Erikson Institute and have been working as a therapist since 2004.
